🧠Data Analyst Agent · Insight
capital-gain is the most statistically pathological column in this dataset. Its median is $0 — 91% of rows have zero capital gains — yet it contains 21 extreme outliers up to $99,999 (8.6× the IQR fence). Standard Min-Max or Z-score scaling will compress 91% of values into a near-zero range, effectively destroying the signal in the 9% of rows with non-zero values. Required decomposition: has_capital_gain = (capital_gain > 0).astype(int) preserves the binary signal, while log1p_capital_gain captures the magnitude for non-zero cases. Used together, these two features will outperform the raw column in virtually any model. The 3 high-age outliers (82–90) are biologically plausible but should be winsorized at 80 to prevent extrapolation artifacts.

occupation and workclass exhibit Missing Not At Random (MNAR) — the missingness itself is a high-signal feature. 94% co-occurrence means these two columns are missing for the same 1,836 individuals — almost certainly self-employed workers who had no formal employer or occupation title to report. Mode imputation would artificially inflate "Prof-specialty" by 5.7% and introduce a systematic professional-worker bias across all downstream models. Correct treatment in three steps: (1) Create is_self_employed = (occupation.isna() & workclass.isna()).astype(int) — this binary feature likely carries high predictive signal for income classification. (2) Fill occupation nulls with "Self-Employed". (3) Fill workclass nulls with "Self-emp-inc". Expected accuracy improvement from MNAR-aware imputation vs. mode imputation: ~2–3% AUC.
