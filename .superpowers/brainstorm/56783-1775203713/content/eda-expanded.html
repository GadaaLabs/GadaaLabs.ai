Key Findings
📌
Income skews right — Median income ($54K) is 15% below mean ($62K), driven by a high-earner tail. The top 5% earn more than the bottom 40% combined.
⚠️
Class imbalance detected — 73% of records fall in the <$50K class vs. 27% in ≥$50K. Any classification model needs SMOTE or class-weight adjustment to avoid biased predictions.
🔗
Age × Income correlation is strong (r = 0.68) — earnings peak in the 45–55 cohort. Younger workers cluster heavily below $40K regardless of education.
🎓
Education drives earnings non-linearly — Bachelor's holders earn 2.1× more than HS graduates on median, but Master's/Doctorate holders earn only 1.3× more than Bachelor's.
🚨
24 outliers flagged in capital-gains — likely data entry errors or genuine high-net-worth individuals. Remove before training or treat as a separate segment.
Recommendations
1. Log-transform income and capital-gains before modeling — both violate normality assumptions.
2. Use education-num (ordinal) instead of education (categorical) — identical information, fewer columns after encoding.
3. Start with XGBoost — dataset size (32K), mixed feature types, and moderate class imbalance are ideal conditions. Expected baseline AUC: 0.87–0.91.
