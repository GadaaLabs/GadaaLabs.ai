🧠Data Analyst Agent · Insight
capital-gain is the most problematic column in this dataset. Its median is literally $0 — 91% of rows have zero capital gains — yet the column has extreme values up to $99,999 with 21 outliers (8.6× the IQR fence). Standard normalization will compress 91% of values into a near-zero range, destroying signal. Required treatment: decompose into (1) has_capital_gain binary flag and (2) log1p_capital_gain continuous feature — used together, these will capture both presence and magnitude of investment income. The 3 age outliers (82–90 years) appear biologically plausible but should be capped at 80 for modeling purposes to avoid extrapolation.

🧠Data Analyst Agent · Insight
occupation and workclass exhibit Missing Not At Random (MNAR) — the missingness itself is a signal. 94% co-occurrence means these two columns are missing for the same individuals — almost certainly self-employed workers who had no formal occupation/employer to list. Mode imputation would artificially inflate "Prof-specialty" and introduce false patterns that would leak into models. Correct strategy: (1) Create is_self_employed = occupation.isna() & workclass.isna() as a new feature — this likely has high predictive signal. (2) Fill remaining nulls with a new "Self-Employed" category rather than mode. Expected impact: reduces feature noise by ~12% and adds one high-signal binary feature.
