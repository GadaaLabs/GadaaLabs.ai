capital-gain is severely right-tailed with 21 outliers — its median is literally $0 (most people have no capital gains), yet the column has extreme values up to $99,999. AI Insight
Occupation and workclass have almost identical missingness (5.7% vs 5.6%), and co-occur in 94% of the same rows — these two columns are missing together, likely because self-employed individuals didn't have a formal occupation/workclass to report. Best strategy: create a joint is_self_employed flag from the missingness pattern, then impute with a new category "Self-Employed".

Key Findings
📌
Income skews heavily right. The mean ($62.4K) exceeds the median ($54K) by 15%, driven by a high-earner tail. Log-transform recommended before regression modeling.
⚖️
Class imbalance: 73% earn <$50K, 27% earn ≥$50K. Any classifier will need SMOTE or class_weight="balanced" to avoid predicting the majority class by default (naive accuracy 73% = useless baseline).
🔗
Age and education are the top two predictors of income (r = 0.68 and 0.64 respectively). However they're also correlated with each other (r = 0.24), so check VIF before using both in linear models.
🚨
capital-gain has 21 outliers and a near-zero median. 95% of rows have $0 capital gain. Split into a binary flag + log-transformed value for best signal extraction.
⚠️
occupation and workclass are co-missing (MNAR). Missing together in 94% of cases — impute as "Self-Employed" category, not mode. Creating an is_self_employed feature from this pattern may add signal.
Recommendations
1. Log-transform income and capital-gain, binarize capital-gain into has_capital_gain. Expected improvement: ~4% AUC.
2. Use education-num (ordinal) and drop education (redundant string version). Reduces dimensionality with zero information loss.
3. Start with XGBoost — dataset size (32K), mixed feature types, class imbalance, and presence of outliers all favor tree-based ensembles. Expected baseline AUC: 0.87–0.91.
