Data science sits at the intersection of statistics, programming, and domain knowledge. Technical practitioners who lack the statistical foundation will misinterpret their models. Those who lack the engineering foundation will never get their models into production. This course gives you both.

You will start with the statistics and probability that underpin every model — not as abstract theory but as practical tools for understanding data distributions, drawing correct inferences, and avoiding common pitfalls like p-hacking and data leakage. You will then move into supervised machine learning: regression, classification, and the evaluation methodology that tells you whether your model will actually generalise. The course closes with a full end-to-end scikit-learn Pipeline that takes raw data to a deployable model.

A working knowledge of Python and Pandas (from the Data Analysis with Python course) is assumed. No prior machine learning experience is required.
