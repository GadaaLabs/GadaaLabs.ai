RAG is the dominant architecture for grounding large language models in proprietary knowledge — but 80% of RAG systems underperform because of poor chunking decisions, weak retrieval, or absent evaluation pipelines. Building a RAG system that reliably returns accurate, grounded answers requires engineering discipline across the full stack: document ingestion, embedding selection, index configuration, query optimisation, and continuous quality measurement.

This course teaches RAG engineering from first principles, not from LangChain abstractions. You will understand why HNSW beats brute-force ANN at scale, why your chunk size choices directly control retrieval recall, and why a cross-encoder reranker can recover relevance that a bi-encoder missed. You will build each component from scratch before using any framework — because understanding the mechanics is the only way to debug production failures.

The course covers the full RAG pipeline: ingesting documents from PDF, HTML, and DOCX sources; designing chunking strategies for different content types; selecting and benchmarking embedding models on your domain data; configuring vector databases for production (filtering, quantisation, index tuning); measuring retrieval quality with Recall@k and NDCG; applying query transformations that improve recall; and evaluating end-to-end quality with the RAGAS framework.

Advanced lessons cover agentic RAG, Corrective RAG, Self-RAG, and graph RAG — the architectures that go beyond simple retrieve-and-generate. The final two lessons address production operations (latency budgets, caching, cost optimisation) and enterprise concerns (multi-tenancy, access control, compliance).
